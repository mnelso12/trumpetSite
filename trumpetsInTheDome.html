Trumpets Under the Dome

best gameday tradition since 1993

Trumpets Under the Dome is the most beautiful display of school tradition, not only at the University of Notre Dame, but at any university in America. Every home football weekend the trumpet section gathers around the circular rings of the Main Building to perform our Alma Mater, “Notre Dame, Our Mother”, and fight song, “The Notre Dame Victory March”. We are greeted by thousands of fans each week who gather to hear us play.

Tradition

The first Trumpets Under the Dome was on November 12, 1993, the night before #2 Notre Dame beat #1 Florida State in the “Game of the Century”. That evening, a handful of trumpets gathered in front of the steps of the Main Building to play. Logically, those trumpets took credit for Notre Dame’s victory, and continued their performance every week. By the next year, the rest of the section had joined in and they had been invited inside the Main Building by an administrative assistant, and began performing literally “under the dome”. Since 1993 the lore and tradition of Trumpets Under the Dome has only grown. Hundreds of band members have participated in the tradition, passing on the legacy left by alumni before them. In this way, and in so many others, Trumpets Under the Dome fully embodies the motto of the Band of the Fighting Irish, Tradition, Excellence, Family.

We sincerely hope no matter who you are that you come to take part in this unique and historic tradition. All floors of the building fill to capacity at nearly every performance, so we recommend that you get there early. Go Irish!

After the last game of this season, the section goes to the Dome to perform one final time. Seniors listen as the underclassmen play the Alma Mater and Victory March to salute them. It is a great way to mark the end of four fantastic years. “Trumpet Under the Dome” takes place every home game weekend under the Golden Dome on Friday at 4:10pm and on game day 65 minutes before kickoff.
